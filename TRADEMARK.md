# Project name and brand

The **code** in this repository is free software under the [MIT License](LICENSE): you may use,
copy, modify, and redistribute it, including for commercial purposes, on the MIT terms. Nothing
here restricts those rights.

This document is about the **name**, not the code. "proofbundle" and "b7n0de" are the identity of
this project and the group that maintains it. They are **not registered trademarks** — there is no ®
claim here; this is a plain, honest use-of-name policy, not a legal notice.

## What is fine (no permission needed)

- Referring to the project by name — "built on proofbundle", "verified with proofbundle", "a
  proofbundle receipt", tutorials, articles, comparisons, talks.
- Stating, truthfully, that your software uses or is compatible with proofbundle.
- Forking the repository under the MIT License and building on it.

## What we ask you not to do

- Do not name a **fork, redistribution, or competing package** "proofbundle" (or a confusingly
  similar name) in a way that implies it is the official project or is endorsed by the maintainer.
  Pick your own name and note "based on proofbundle" if you like.
- Do not publish a package to PyPI (or any registry) under the name `proofbundle` other than the
  official releases.
- Do not use the "b7n0de" name or logo to suggest official status, endorsement, or affiliation you
  do not have.

## Why this exists

proofbundle's whole value is that a receipt means what it says. A fork published under the same name
could ship a weaker verifier while inheriting the trust in the name — the one thing this project
cannot allow. Keeping the name tied to the reviewed, gated releases protects users, not the
maintainer's ego. If in doubt, ask in an issue; reasonable requests are welcome.
